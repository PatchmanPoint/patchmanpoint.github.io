Perspective

Small, free, and still taking a step ahead

June 5, 2026

Patchman is still young, but that is exactly why user freedom matters so much. Being small does not mean standing still. It means moving carefully, keeping the product honest, and making sure developers can use it freely without feeling trapped.

As we jump into the community with the introduction of Patchman, some people ask for comparisons with other apps. That is fantastic. It means the project is being noticed. Still, Patchman is in an early release and has been around for less than a month, so comparing it directly with mature apps can feel like asking a small project to prove itself against products that have had years to grow.

Even so, being small is not something to worry about. A small product can still take a step ahead when the direction is clear. For Patchman, that direction is simple: protect the freedom of the user, keep the workflow practical, and make API testing easier without putting the basics behind a paywall.

Freedom first

There are many mature apps for API testing, and a lot of them offer strong features. Many also come with subscription limits that shape how you organize your work. Patchman takes a different approach. It is free to use, and really free in the part that matters most to everyday development.

You can create workspaces freely, group them in a way that makes sense for your projects, and manage them inside your repository without artificial limits. That means your API work can stay close to your codebase, your branches, and your team workflow instead of being gated by a pricing tier.

  • Create workspaces without worrying about subscription caps.
  • Group requests in a structure that matches your own projects.
  • Manage collections in your repository with a workflow that stays under your control.

Not here to replace everyone

Patchman is not trying to kill or replace other apps. The goal is to join the community and help developers solve their cases more smoothly. If another tool already works for you, that is fine. Patchman simply offers another path for people who want a lighter, more open way to work.

You can use Patchman across your environments: local, staging, production, or wherever your workflow needs to go. The app may still be early, but the intention is serious. Step by step, it will continue growing while keeping the same promise: the user should stay free.

So yes, Patchman is still small. Maybe too small to be compared fairly with mature API testing apps today. But that is not the end of the story. Sooner or later, Patchman will grow big enough to stand in those comparisons. Until then, the important part is staying useful, staying honest, and staying truly free.
